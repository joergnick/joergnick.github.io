About Me

I am a mathematician specializing in numerical analysis, partial differential equations, and scientific machine learning. My research focuses on developing efficient numerical methods for wave phenomena and the mathematical foundations of metamaterials. I completed my doctorate at the University of Tübingen, where my work was part of the Collaborative Research Center (CRC) 1173, focusing on time-dependent boundary integral equations for wave scattering problems. Currently, I am a Post-Doc at the Seminar of Applied Mathematics (SAM) at ETH Zürich, where I explore the mathematical properties of materials with high refractive index.

Research Interests

  • Numerical analysis of partial differential equations
  • Computational methods for wave phenomena
  • Boundary integral equations
  • Scientific machine learning applications in mathematics
  • Mathematical foundations of metamaterials
